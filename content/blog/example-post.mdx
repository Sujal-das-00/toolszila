## Overview

California payroll is shaped by federal withholding, FICA, and state income tax rules. This article explains how those pieces interact and how to estimate net pay without manually building tax tables.

## What affects California take-home pay?

Your net paycheck depends on salary, pay frequency, filing status, pre-tax deductions, federal withholding, state withholding, Social Security, and Medicare.

- Review your withholding after a raise or bonus.
- Compare net pay across pay frequencies when budgeting.
- Track state-specific withholding separately from federal withholding.

## FAQ

### How is California paycheck tax calculated?

California paycheck tax combines federal income tax withholding, California state income tax withholding, Social Security tax, and Medicare tax.

### Does California tax bonuses differently?

Bonuses may be withheld differently from regular wages depending on payroll handling, but they are still part of your taxable compensation.

### Can I estimate take-home pay from salary alone?

Yes. A salary-based estimate is usually enough for planning, though actual payroll can differ when deductions or employer benefits change.
