# ToolsZila — Ireland Market Keyword Strategy, Clustering & Blog Plan

**Prepared for:** toolszila.com (expansion from US → Ireland)
**Target market:** Republic of Ireland (English-language, EUR, Revenue.ie tax system)
**Tax year focus:** 2026
**Goal:** Build visibility fast via low-competition long-tail + topical authority, while passing AdSense content review.

> **How to read difficulty/volume here:** Exact search volumes require a paid tool (Ahrefs/Semrush/Keyword Planner) tied to your account — numbers below are *qualitative bands* based on observed demand, SERP competition, and competitor coverage. Validate the starred "priority" terms in your own tool before committing resources. Bands: **Low / Med / High** competition; **Low / Med / High** volume.

---

## 1. The Core Strategic Insight

Two traffic engines, tightly interlinked:

1. **Tool pages (transactional)** — capture people who already want to calculate.
2. **Blog/info pages (informational)** — capture the larger, lower-competition pool searching "is €X a good salary", "average salary Ireland", "how much tax do I pay". These rank faster, build topical authority that lifts the money pages, and produce the substantive content AdSense requires.

**The golden internal link:** every informational page routes the reader into a matching calculator (`"is €50k a good salary"` → `/50000-after-tax-ireland`).

| salaryaftertaxie.com | Has "good salary" + profession blog content | Limited tool depth; beatable on calculator UX + freshness |
| nettocalc.com, incomecalculator.ie, purpletree.ie | Functional calculators | Pure tools, almost no surrounding content (weak on long-tail + AdSense-style depth) |
| Morgan McKinley, recruiters | Salary-guide authority | Gated PDFs, no free after-tax calc, no long-tail capture |

### The three big gaps (your opportunity)
1. **No dedicated per-salary pages.** Competitors answer "€50k after tax" inside a calculator, not on a `/50000-after-tax-ireland` URL. Exact-match pages = fast long-tail wins.
2. **No dedicated per-profession pages.** Everyone dumps "nurse / teacher / software engineer salary" into one shared table. A standalone "Nurse Salary Ireland After Tax 2026" page with its own calculator + context is wide open.
3. **Weak "is €X a good salary" + regional (Dublin vs Cork) content.** High intent, high volume, thin/generic competition. This is your fastest informational win.

## 5. Keyword Clustering Architecture

Each cluster = 1 pillar + supporting spokes. Spokes link to pillar + siblings; pillar links down to spokes + across to the most relevant tool page. **No page without an internal link into a calculator.**

### Cluster A — Take-Home Pay (TRANSACTIONAL pillar)
- **Pillar:** `/ireland-take-home-pay-calculator` (hub)
- **Spokes:** all `€X-after-tax-ireland` pages (30k–100k)
- Links: each spoke → pillar + 3–4 neighbouring amounts

### Cluster B — Irish Tax Explained (INFO pillar — YMYL trust)
- **Pillar:** "Irish Income Tax 2026: PAYE, USC & PRSI Explained"
- **Spokes:** PAYE explained · USC rates 2026 · PRSI explained · tax credits Ireland · standard rate cut-off point · emergency tax · Budget 2026 changes
- Links: every spoke → take-home calculator

### Cluster C — Salary Benchmarking (INFO pillar — FASTEST traction)
- **Pillar:** "Average Salary in Ireland 2026"
- **Spokes:** is €40k/€50k/€60k/€70k a good salary · average salary Dublin · minimum wage 2026 · average salary by age/sector
- Links: each "good salary" spoke → matching `€X-after-tax` tool page (**highest-value link on the site**)

### Cluster D — Professions (HYBRID — biggest gap)
- **Pillar:** "Salary in Ireland by Profession (After Tax) 2026"
- **Spokes (own page each):** nurse · teacher · software engineer · garda · civil service · data analyst · cyber security · pharmacist
- Each spoke = short calculator pre-filled to the typical salary + context + after-tax breakdown → links to matching `€X-after-tax` page

### Cluster E — Decisions & Comparisons (INFO pillar)
- **Pillar:** "Cost of Living in Ireland 2026"
- **Spokes:** Dublin vs Cork · Ireland vs UK take-home · how much rent can I afford · moving to Ireland salary guide

### Cluster F — High-CPC Reliefs (COMMERCIAL — ad revenue)
- Standalone posts: pension tax relief · salary sacrifice · work-from-home relief · rent tax credit · bonus tax

## 6. Blog Writing Strategy

### Publishing sequence (build cluster-by-cluster for topical depth)
| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Why first |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1–2 | Cluster C "is €X a good salary" (40k, 50k, 60k) | Lowest difficulty, high intent, instant links to new tool pages |
| 2 | 3–4 | Cluster B tax-explainer pillar + USC/PRSI/PAYE/emergency-tax spokes | YMYL trust backbone + emergency tax = high volume |
| 3 | 5–6 | Cluster C pillar "Average Salary Ireland 2026" + benchmarks | Builds authority, internal-link hub |
| 4 | 7–8 | Cluster D profession pages | Exploits biggest competitor gap |
| 5 | Ongoing | Cluster E comparisons + Cluster F high-CPC reliefs | Monetization + breadth |
| — | Oct & Jan | Refresh ALL year-stamped pages | Budget + new tax year freshness |

### Per-post template
1. **Title:** exact keyword + "2026" + "Ireland".
2. **First 2 sentences = the answer** (snippet / AI-overview bait). Give the verdict, then the nuance.
3. **One credible data point early** — cite CSO (salary figures) or Revenue.ie (tax). Write facts in your own words.
4. **A worked after-tax example** that embeds or links the matching calculator with that exact salary.
5. **FAQ block** with `FAQPage` JSON-LD schema.
6. **Sources + disclaimer** ("estimates, not financial advice") — YMYL trust + AdSense compliance.
7. **Internal links:** pillar + 2–3 siblings + 1 calculator.

### What lets you outrank thin competitors
- **Specificity + freshness:** exact CSO figures (median ~€38k, average ~€49–53k), updated each quarter — beats evergreen mush.
- **Regional angle:** Dublin pays ~10–20% more but costs 30–50% more to live; outside Dublin €45k+ ≈ Dublin €60k lifestyle. Under-told, high-interest.
- **Newcomer/relocation angle:** big search volume from people moving to Ireland for tech; weakly covered; high-CPC.
- **Dedicated pages > shared tables:** one URL per profession / per salary, where competitors use one crammed table.

### Data points to reuse (verified 2026)
- Median full-time salary ≈ **€38,000**; mean ≈ **€44–53,000** (CSO, two-speed economy).
- Minimum wage **€14.15/hr** from Jan 2026.
- Tax: 20% to **€44,000** (single), 40% above; credits **€4,000** (single PAYE).
- USC: 0.5% / 2% / 3% / 8% across bands; exempt ≤ €13,000 (cliff edge).
- PRSI Class A **4.2%** (→ 4.35% from 1 Oct 2026); none below €352/week.
- Dublin "comfortable single" ≈ **€60,000+**; outside Dublin ≈ **€45,000+**.
