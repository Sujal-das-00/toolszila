# ToolsZila — New Zealand Market Keyword Strategy, Clustering & Blog Plan

**Prepared for:** toolszila.com (second expansion — Ireland launched, now NZ)
**Target market:** New Zealand (English-language, NZD, Inland Revenue PAYE system)
**Tax year focus:** 2026/27 (1 April 2026 – 31 March 2027)
**Goal:** Replicate Ireland's success with faster, simpler tax engine and less competition.

---

## 1. Why New Zealand First (After Ireland)

NZ is the ideal second-market expansion:
- **Simpler tax system than Ireland.** PAYE brackets (10.5%–39%) + ACC levy (1.75%) + optional KiwiSaver. No USC cliff-edges, no credit complexity. You can build it in a day.
- **Lower SERP competition.** Fewer dedicated calculators (paycalculator.co.nz, moneybalance.co.nz exist, but salaryaftertax.com/nz is thin and dated). The "$X after tax NZ" long-tail is nearly empty.
- **English language, reusable content.** Copy-paste your entire Ireland blog playbook, swap the numbers.
- **High-income, tech-savvy audience.** Median salary ~NZ$69,836; tech/finance hubs in Auckland/Wellington. Will click ads, will search salary calculators.

| Xero NZ, Inland Revenue | Authority | Focused on business/compliance, not salary-after-tax content |

### The three big gaps
1. **No dedicated "$X after tax NZ" pages.** A `/60000-after-tax-nz` page is literally not in the SERP. Exact-match URL + H1 = fast win.
2. **No "is $X a good salary in NZ" content.** Very high intent, zero dedicated coverage. "Is $60,000 a good salary in NZ?" has one thin competitor page.
3. **No Auckland vs regional salary angle.** NZ has huge regional variance (Auckland ~15–20% higher pay, but also higher COL). This angle is completely under-served.

## 5. Keyword Clustering Architecture

Same model as Ireland: 1 pillar + supporting spokes. Spokes link to pillar + siblings; pillar links down to spokes + across to the most relevant tool page. **No page without a calculator link.**

### Cluster A — Take-Home Pay (TRANSACTIONAL pillar)
- **Pillar:** `/nz-take-home-pay-calculator` (hub)
- **Spokes:** all `$X-after-tax-nz` pages (30k–120k)
- Links: each spoke → pillar + 3–4 neighbouring amounts

### Cluster B — NZ Tax Explained (INFO pillar — YMYL trust)
- **Pillar:** "New Zealand PAYE Explained: Tax Brackets, ACC & KiwiSaver 2026/27"
- **Spokes:** PAYE brackets explained · ACC levy explained · KiwiSaver tax · student loan repayment · tax brackets 2026 · how much tax do I pay
- Links: every spoke → take-home calculator

### Cluster C — Salary Benchmarking (INFO pillar — FASTEST traction)
- **Pillar:** "Average Salary in New Zealand 2026/27"
- **Spokes:** is $40k/$60k/$80k a good salary · average salary auckland · minimum wage 2026 · average salary by age/sector
- Links: each "good salary" spoke → matching `$X-after-tax` tool page (**highest-value link on site**)

### Cluster D — Professions (HYBRID — biggest gap)
- **Pillar:** "Salary in New Zealand by Profession (After Tax) 2026"
- **Spokes (own page each):** software engineer · nurse · teacher · plumber/electrician/tradies · healthcare · finance
- Each spoke = short calc pre-filled to typical salary + context + after-tax breakdown → links to matching `$X-after-tax` page

### Cluster E — Regional / Decisions (INFO pillar)
- **Pillar:** "Cost of Living by Region in New Zealand 2026"
- **Spokes:** auckland vs wellington vs christchurch · auckland salary guide · regional salary expectations

### Cluster F — Immigration / Relocation (INFO pillar)
- **Pillar:** "Moving to New Zealand: Salary Expectations & Work Visa Requirements 2026"

### Cluster G — High-CPC Reliefs (COMMERCIAL — ad revenue)

## 6. Blog Writing Strategy

### Publishing sequence (cluster-by-cluster)
| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Why first |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1–2 | Cluster C "is $X a good salary" (40k, 60k, 80k) | Lowest difficulty, high intent, instant links to new tool pages |
| 2 | 3–4 | Cluster B PAYE/ACC/KiwiSaver explainers | YMYL trust backbone |
| 3 | 5–6 | Cluster C pillar "Average Salary NZ 2026" + benchmarks | Authority hub |
| 4 | 7–8 | Cluster E regional salary comparisons | Exploits unique NZ angle |
| 5 | 9–10 | Cluster D profession pages | Biggest gap |
| 6 | 11+ | Cluster F immigration + Cluster G high-CPC | Completeness + monetization |
| — | Apr & Oct | Refresh all year-stamped pages | Tax year changes (1 April) + mid-year updates |

### Per-post template (same as Ireland)
1. **Title:** exact keyword + "2026" or "2026/27" (NZ tax year).
2. **First 2 sentences = the answer** (snippet bait). Verdict first, nuance second.
3. **One credible data point early** — cite Stats NZ (salary figures) or Inland Revenue (tax). Write in your own words.
4. **Worked after-tax example** embedding or linking the matching calculator.
5. **FAQ block** with `FAQPage` JSON-LD schema.
6. **Sources + disclaimer** ("estimates, not financial advice").
7. **Internal links:** pillar + 2–3 siblings + 1 calculator.

### What lets you outrank thin competitors
- **Specificity + freshness:** exact Stats NZ figures (median $69,836, mean $81,484), updated quarterly — beats generic mush.
- **Regional angle:** Auckland pays 15–20% more but costs 30–50% more to live; provincial towns deliver same lifestyle on $15k less. This is under-told, high-interest.
- **Tech/immigration angle:** NZ has skilled-migration paths (Green List, Skilled Migrant Category); searchers want to know "is my $X salary enough to move?". Weak coverage.
- **Dedicated pages > shared tables:** one page per profession / per salary, not one crammed table.

### Data points to reuse (verified 2026/27)
- Median salary ≈ **NZ$69,836**; mean ≈ **NZ$81,484** (Stats NZ, June 2025 data).
- Minimum wage **NZ$23.95/hr** from 1 April 2026.
- Immigration median wage **NZ$35/hr** (NZ$72,800/year) from 9 March 2026 (visa threshold).
- PAYE brackets: 10.5% to $15,600 · 17.5% to $53,500 · 30% to $78,100 · 33% to $180,000 · 39% above.
- ACC earner levy **1.75%** on earnings up to **NZ$156,641** from 1 April 2026.
- KiwiSaver default **3%** employee + 3% employer (rising to 3.5% each from 1 April 2026).
- Student loan repayments **12%** of income above NZ$24,128.
- Auckland average ≈ **NZ$80–114k** (depending on source); regional ≈ **15–20% lower**.
- No tax-free threshold — every dollar is taxed.

- **Year-stamped term impressions** after Apr/Oct refreshes.

## 8. Timeline & Realistic Expectations

- **Weeks 1–4:** long-tail `$X after tax NZ` + "is $X a good salary" pages should start ranking (thin exact-match competition).
- **Weeks 5–8:** info pillars + PAYE explainers mature; topical authority compounds.
- **Months 2–3:** regional and profession pages gain traction.
- **Month 3+:** head terms ("PAYE calculator NZ") become reachable as authority builds.

> **NZ market size caveat:** NZ population is 5M vs Ireland's 5.1M, but only ~2.85M are workers (Stats NZ). Expect 40–60% of the absolute traffic volume of Ireland, BUT the opportunity is higher because the SERP is thinner and competitor-free. Win rate on long-tail should be faster.
